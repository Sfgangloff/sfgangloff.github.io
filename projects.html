A description of my main research project can be found there. It is related to computational threshold phenomena related to lattice models and statistical physics. Here I would like to describe other problems I am interested in my work as an academic researcher but also some more free thoughts that I found as well (sometimes more) valuable.

Academic research:

Conjectures:

I got interested in symbolic dynamical systems at the end of my master studies through some insanely hard unsolved problems such as Collatz conjecture and the Langton ant. The symbolic version of the first one (through a coding in base two for instance) as well as the second one can be seen as precise statements over long terms behaviors of some symbolic dynamical systems. My interpretation is that they involve a lot of re-writting and understanding their global behavior takes up a lot of information and computations; these computations depend on the initial configuration of the system and can not be done globally, even partially. I am also interested on other conjectures related to dynamics, computation, and statistical physics: Lonnely runner, Černý, and Furstenberg's x2x3 conjectures ; a conjecture of B. Weiss which states that a multidimensional sofic subshift has a SFT cover with same entropy, the problem of the connective constant of the square lattice.

Formal:

1. Tridimensional SFT: is the entropy of a block gluing tridimensional SFT computable ? is there an aperiodic one ? with undecidable language ?

2. What is the entropy of the Kari-Culik tiling? of square ice ?

3. What are the values of entropy for bidimensional block gluing SFT?

Unformal:

1. Classification of local rules types involved in the description of multidimensional SFT (signals, structures, random bits, etc.): these words are used, but there is no formal object behind them which are defined in the literature. There is a first attempt to do this in my Ph.D. thesis (second chapter).

2. Mesuring the organization property of dynamical systems: the entropy is not adapted since it has high values for highly chaotic systems and low values for very structures ones. Is there some natural invariant which has high values for "organized" systems and low ones for highly chaotic or structured ones?
