Conjectures:

I got interested in symbolic dynamical systems at the end of my master studies through some insanely hard unsolved problems such as Collatz conjecture and the Langton ant. The symbolic version of the first one (through a coding in base two for instance) as well as the second one can be seen as precise statements over long terms behaviors of some symbolic dynamical systems. My interpretation is that they involve a lot of re-writting and understanding their global behavior takes up a lot of information and computations; these computations depend on the initial configuration of the system and can not be done globally, even partially. I am also interested on other conjectures related to dynamics, computation, and statistical physics: Lonnely runner, Černý, and Furstenberg's x2x3 conjectures ; a conjecture of B. Weiss which states that a multidimensional sofic subshift has a SFT cover with same entropy, the problem of the connective constant of the square lattice.
