<p class="text_container">
The following texts are related to a project to understand and extend 
             some methods developped in statistical mechanics in order to compute exactly the topological entropy 
             of some simple multidimensional SFT (called lattice models in this field). 

	<br>
	<br>
  
	- <B>On the entropy of square ice</B>: <a href="https://journals.aps.org/pr/abstract/10.1103/PhysRev.162.162">
    	Residual entropy of square ice</a>, E.H. Lieb.

	<br>
    	<br>
    
	- Existence of solution of Bethe equations: <a href="https://journals.aps.org/pr/abstract/10.1103/PhysRev.150.321">
    	One-Dimensional Chain of Anisotropic Spin-Spin Interactions. I. 
    	Proof of Bethe's Hypothesis for Ground State in a Finite System</a>, C.N. Yang, C.P. Yang.

	<br>
    	<br>
             
	- Asymptotic consideration on Bethe roots and ground state: <a href="https://journals.aps.org/pr/abstract/10.1103/PhysRev.150.327">
    	One-Dimensional Chain of Anisotropic Spin-Spin Interactions. II. 
    	Properties of the Ground-State Energy Per Lattice Site for an Infinite System</a>, C.N. Yang, C.P. Yang.

	<br>
    	<br>
        
	- Diagonalisation of the XY model: <a href="https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/0003491661901154">
    	Two soluble models of an antiferromagnetic chain</a>, E.H. Lieb, T. Schultz and D. Mattis.

	<br>
    	<br>
        
  	- Exposition of the coordinate Bethe ansatz: <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1611.09909">
